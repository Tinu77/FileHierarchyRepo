Atinuke Sobowale is a passionate and service-driven IT professional with a strong background in public sector innovation, digital communication, and community development. She holds a Bachelor’s in Information and Communications Technology from Crawford University and a Master’s in Information Technology from the University of Lagos. With certifications in Oracle, Java, and project management, she blends technical expertise with a deep commitment to social impact. As a web administrator at the Bureau of Information Technology in Ogun State, Atinuke has led digital transformation projects across various ministries, especially in public health and education. Her work during the cholera outbreak and her proactive use of ChatGPT for government training sessions reflect her forward-thinking approach to technology and governance. Beyond her professional life, Atinuke is an active volunteer in her church, mentoring teenagers through essay and debate competitions. Her leadership in the NYSC HIV/AIDS Unit and her outreach with the Nigerian Christian Corpers’ Fellowship’s *Rural Rugged* program highlight her lifelong dedication to peacebuilding, health education, and faith-based service. She is a member of the Nigeria Computer Society and a certified IT professional with the Computer Professionals Registration Council of Nigeria. With strong communication skills, a heart for youth empowerment, and a vision for tech-enabled peacebuilding, Atinuke is a strong candidate for the Rotary Peace Fellowship and a changemaker in the making.
